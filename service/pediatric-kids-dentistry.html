Why is a Pediatric Dentist Important?

Preventing Tooth Decay in Early Years for Healthy Teeth in Adulthood!
  • Catch Problems Early: Potential problems can be detected early and treated easily. This prevents bigger and complicated issues that need invasive procedures.
  • Build Healthy Habits: Teach children & parents, the importance of oral hygiene for lifelong benefits. Dental hygiene easily becomes a part of life.
  • Positive Experiences: Pediatric dentists have specialized training in caring for children and creating positive care experience. This reduces a childâ€™s anxiety associated with dental treatments.
Facial Growth and Development of Your Child
  • Dentofacial Orthopedic care is usually initiated during childhood or early adolescence when jaw bones are still growing.
  • This is essential as it guides the facial bone growth and achieve appropriate facial structure and function.
  • Headgears, palatal expanders, twin block, Herbst appliance are usually used to assist with better jaw alignment.
Jaw and Teeth Alignment
  • Diagnosis, prevention, and correction of misaligned teeth and jaws is important to optimal development of teeth and jaws.
  • Orthodontic treatment corrects bad bites â€“ overbite, underbite, crossbite, open bite, addresses crowding or spacing of teeth and dental arch issues.
  • Braces, aligners, retainers are commonly used to align the teeth.
