Oral Medicine & Radiology

Oral Medicine and Radiology is a specialized branch of dentistry that focuses on the diagnosis and non-surgical management of and the interpretation of imaging of mouth, jaws, face, and neck. It plays a critical role in identifying complex oral conditions and guiding treatment planning, often in collaboration with other specialties.

  • Oral medicine deals with diagnosis and management of mucosal lesions, chronic orofacial pain, salivary gland disorders, precancerous and cancerous lesions of the mouth, and medically compromised patients in the dental setting.
  • Oral radiology uses dental and craniofacial imaging to detect pathologies such as cysts, tumors and support treatment planning for implants, surgeries, orthodontics, and endodontics

Digital Intra Oral Scan

A digital intraoral scan is a modern dental imaging technique that captures detailed 3D images of a patient's teeth and gums using a handheld device called an intraoral scanner. It replaces traditional dental impressions (which use messy materials like alginate or silicone) with a quicker, cleaner, and more comfortable process.

Key Features:
  • Non-invasive: No physical impression trays required.
  • High accuracy: Produces highly detailed 3D models.
  • Fast turnaround: Digital files can be sent instantly to dental labs.
  • Patient comfort: No gagging or discomfort from impression materials.
  • Used for: Crowns, bridges, aligners (like Invisalign), implants, and diagnostic purposes.

Aligner Planning

Aligner planning is the digital process of designing and sequencing clear aligners (like Invisalign or ClearCorrect) to gradually move teeth into desired positions. It's a key part of clear aligner therapy, combining 3D scanning, orthodontic diagnostics, and CAD software to create a custom treatment plan.

Benefits of Digital Aligner Planning:
  • Highly customized treatments
  • Predictable outcomes
  • Fewer in-office visits
  • Clear visualization for patients and clinicians
  • Fast turnaround from scan to treatment start
